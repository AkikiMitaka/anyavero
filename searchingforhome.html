Searching For Home

Exploring Identity, Displacement, and Spiritual Shelter

Searching For Home reflects on the meaning of belonging in the modern world. Increasingly, our identities are blurred, we explore new horizons and places to settle, in search of a place and state we can call home. It’s not just about the physical location but also the choice of vocation, the meaning to one’s life and the sense of belonging to certain communities and people.

A Copper Road Heading To The Spectacular

Oil on Faux Silk | 180 x 130 cm | 2025

The copper road symbolizes a destiny or a pathway that must be followed, even if its destination is grand but uncertain. The use of copper tones grounds the painting in earthly, foundational elements, contrasted with the ephemeral nature of the abstract sky.

A Radar Looming Over The Rolling Hills

Oil on Faux Silk | 155 x 131 cm | 2025

This piece captures the tension between natural beauty (the rolling hills) and constant vigilance (the radar). It reflects the artist’s history in a post-Cold War landscape, where the search for personal peace is overshadowed by historical tension and the need for guidance and connection.

Pathways Cradling Calm And Adventure

Oil on Faux Silk | 120 x 120 cm | 2024

The central theme here is duality: the desire for stability (calm) battling the necessity of movement and change (adventure). The texture of the silk brings a shifting light that suggests memory and movement, vital elements in defining one's home.
